Survival Analysis

Objective

To analyze or predict when an event is likely to happen. For ESASE, we used the Survival Analysis to predict which employee had a major probability to quit the job.

Variables

Survival Analysis data-points are feed with 3 elements:

  1. dummy E=1, if an event happens and Ei= 0 in case of censoring.
  2. T observed time as E=0 until the present or E=1
  3. X (vector of featuresin this case, we have a dataframe with 450 rows and 20 columns (features).

Conditional Survival Forest Model

In order to identify the variables behind the dynamic showed by survival functions, we ran a Conditional Survival Forest Model. The concordance index or C-Index represent modelsâ€™ ability to correctly provide a reliable ranking of the survival times based on the individual risk scores C index = 1 corresponds to the best model prediction, and C index =0.5 represents a random prediction.
  • Our model reaches a C-index of .73
The Integrated Brier Score (IBS) provides an overall calculation of the model performance at all available times. Is always a number between 0 and 1, with 0 being the best possible value.
  • Our model is behind .25 for all times predicted

XGBOOST

Function Objective: Training Loss + Regularization

The regularization term is what people usually forget to add. The regularization term controls the complexity of the model, which helps us to avoid overfitting. The general principle is we want both a simple and predictive model. The tradeoff between the two is also referred as bias-variance tradeoff in machine learning.
